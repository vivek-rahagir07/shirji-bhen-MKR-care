From Paste to Liquid Precision: A Scientific Shift

For over a century, the world has relied on thick, abrasive pastes to clean teeth. But as science advances, we're discovering that mechanical scrubbing is only half the battle. The future lies in liquid precision.

The Ancient Beginning

Ancient civilizations used everything from crushed eggshells and pumice to herbal twigs. The primary goal was abrasive cleaning‚Äîliterally scraping away contaminants. While effective to a degree, these methods often damaged delicate tooth enamel.

The Rise of the 100-Year-Old Paste

Modern toothpaste as we know it emerged in the late 19th century. It introduced chemical cleaners and fluoride, moving beyond simple abrasion. However, the delivery system remained the same: a thick paste that requires vigorous mechanical action and often fails to reach the hidden 40% of our oral surface area‚Äîthe gaps between teeth and the gum line.
